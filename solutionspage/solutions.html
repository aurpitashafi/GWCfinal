How to Help
There are multiple ways to help make a difference by donating money or your time. Click through below to see how you can bring an end to world poverty.

The Borgen Project

The Borgen Project is a nonprofit organization that is working to end poverty and hunger by working with US leaders to improve their response to the global poverty crisis. The Borgen Project is considered one of the most politically influential organizations fighting for the world’s poor as they have access to most members of Congress and has a advocacy network of volunteers in every state.

Global Giving

Global Giving is a charity fundraising website that allows nonprofits from across the globe to raise money that is needed in order to improve their community. They have supported 10,252 projects as well as raided $114,889,647 from 392,257 donors. Global Giving allows you to choose a project that you feel passionate about and donate to them.

Donate here!

UNICEF

UNICEF is one of the largest nonprofit organizations and is dedicated to helping children in need. They aim to protect and defend the rights of every child. They work with the United Nations in order to make sure that children are on the global agenda.

Donate here!
Learn more about ways you can help through UNICEF here!

Shot@Life

Shot@Life aims to put an end to global poverty by eradicating one facet of it. They are a nonprofit that provides vaccinations for the less fortunate in order to prevent them from being plagued by diseases that are preventable.

Donate here!
Learn more about ways you can help through Shot@Life here!

International Rescue Committee

In order to prevent global poverty, this nonprofit responds to the world’s most humanitarian crises and helps people to survive and rebuild their lives. Since the founding of this organization in 1933, they have since offered lifesaving care and life-changing assistance to refugees forced to flee from war or disaster.

Donate here!
Learn more about ways you can help through the International Rescue Committee here!

Peace Corps

Peace Corps is a service opportunity that allows activated changemakers to immerse themselves in a community abroad. Peace Corps volunteers work at the grassroot level in order to create change that lasts long after their service.

Find out if Peace Corps is right for you here!

Cross-Cultural Solutions

Cross-Cultural Solutions is a nonprofit working to address global issues by providing volunteer service to communities abroad, and contributing responsibly to local economies. They find cross-cultural ways to combat poverty.

Learn more about them here!

Developing World Connections

Developing World Connections is a non-profit, charitable organization that seeks to connect people and resources through global community development in order to achieve a globally engaged, poverty free, and socially just world. They support community-led projects driven by local needs and priorities as determined by our in-country partner organizations that aim to empower communities, promote human rights, alleviate poverty in order to guarentee an enduring legacy for present and future generations.

Ali Forney Center

The Ali Forney Center, places homeless LGBT youth (up to 40 percent of New York’s homeless youngsters are queer-identified) in apartments overseen by on-site counselors throughout the city. The center also has medical care, counseling, education programs and employment assistance.

Bowery Mission

Founded in 1879 on the original Skid Row, this venerable organization aims to transform the lives of homeless people caught in the cycle of poverty.In addition to providing the immediate needs of food, shelter and clothing, the Mission also offers recovery programs and a free medical clinic.

Address: 227 Bowery between Rivington and Stanton Sts

BronxWorks

BronxWorks’ Homeless Outreach Team (HOT) is on the streets 24/7 to help homeless individuals secure long-term housing, medical and psychiatric services, and referrals to drug and alcohol treatment programs.

Contact the Program Director, Jaun Rivera, at 646-393-4072 or jrivera@bronxworks.org.

Coalition for the Homeless

Each night, 1,000 people who would otherwise go hungry are served a meal thanks to the 35-year-old institution, which also functions as a job-training hub and a crisis-intervention center for at-risk families.

To volunteer, email volunteer@cfthomeless.org.

The Doe Fund

This organization assists formerly homeless and incarcerated adults in becoming self-sufficient by helping them find permanent housing and gain skills needed to rejoin the workforce. Volunteers with hiring or professional experience can conduct mock interviews during two-hour sessions, after which you’ll provide feedback over a dinner prepared by the Doe Fund’s Culinary Arts Program participants. Those interested in a more regular commitment can work one-on-one with trainees for two hours per week to help them achieve their GEDs or focus on career development and résumé building.

Contact info: 212-628-5207
